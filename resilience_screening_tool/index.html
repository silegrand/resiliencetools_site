ASSISTIV

A conversation
about how you
are getting on

Not a test. Not a form.
A gentle, private conversation — at your own pace, in your own words.

What this is
ASSISTIV listens to you talk about your everyday life — how you get around, how you are feeling, who you have around you. There are no forms to fill in. Just speak, in your own words, and the tool will do the rest. It takes around ten to fifteen minutes.
What this is not
This is not a medical test, and no decisions will be made about you on the basis of what you share. There are no right or wrong answers — only your experience.
One thing worth saying before we begin
Many people find it difficult to say how things really are — whether it is not wanting to worry someone they love, or worrying about what might happen as a result. That is a deeply human and completely understandable feeling.

The people who care about you simply want you to be well. And if a little support would help with that, they would almost certainly want that for you — and would not mind at all.

Nothing here leads anywhere without your permission. You are the author of your own life. This conversation is simply here to help you think things through and make the decisions you want — without fear, without pressure, and entirely on your own terms.
Your information is yours
Nothing is saved and nothing is shared without your permission. At the end of the conversation, you choose exactly what happens with what you have told us. Whatever you decide, you will receive a personal Wellness Guide — yours to keep.

You can stop at any point. There is no pressure and no obligation.

One more thing to consider

Would you like to involve someone who cares about you?

You know better than anyone what you need to stay well at home. But sometimes the people who love us notice things that are really useful for a health team to understand — not to make decisions without you, but to make sure any support fits your life.

Everyone involved wants the same thing for you

The people who care about you, and the health team, all want you to be happy, well, and at home on your own terms. Involving someone you trust helps services understand exactly what support would make the biggest difference — for your life, in your way.

If you'd like, we can send a short questionnaire to someone you trust. Their answers and yours stay completely separate. You won't see theirs and they won't see yours — it simply gives the health team a fuller picture.
